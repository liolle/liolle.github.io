Tim Berners-Lee British scientist Alternate titles: Sir Tim Berners-Lee By Michael Aaron Dennis Last Updated: Dec 2, 2022 Edit History Tim Berners-Lee Tim Berners-Lee See all media Born: June 8, 1955 (age 67) London England Founder: World Wide Web Consortium Inventions: World Wide Web World Wide Web Tim Berners-Lee, in full Sir Tim Berners-Lee, (born June 8, 1955, London , England), British computer scientist, generally credited as the inventor of the World Wide Web . In 2004 he was awarded a knighthood by Queen Elizabeth II of the United Kingdom and the inaugural Millennium Technology Prize (â‚¬1 million) by the Finnish Technology Award Foundation.

Computing came naturally to Berners-Lee, as both of his parents worked on the Ferranti Mark I , the first commercial computer. (See computer: The first stored-program machines.) After graduating in 1976 from the University of Oxford, Berners-Lee designed computer software for two years at Plessey Telecommunications Ltd., located in Poole , Dorset, England . Following this, he had several positions in the computer industry, including a stint from June to December 1980 as a software engineering consultant at CERN , the European particle physics laboratory in Geneva.
